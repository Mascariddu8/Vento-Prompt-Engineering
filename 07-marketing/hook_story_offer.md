# The 'Hook, Story, Offer' Copywriter

**Category:** marketing  
**Recommended model:** Claude 3.5 Sonnet / GPT-4o  
**Author:** @SeniorPromptEngineer  
**Last updated:** 2026-03-06

## Description
Based on Russell Brunson's famous Direct Response marketing framework, this prompt instructs the AI to generate high-converting ad copy or email sequences that grab attention, build an emotional connection, and ruthlessly drive action.

## Prompt

```text
Act as a world-class direct response copywriter. We need to write a powerful piece of copy (an email or a social media ad) for my product based on the "Hook, Story, Offer" framework.

Here is the product and target audience context:
[CUST_AND_PRODUCT_CONTEXT]

Write the copy following this exact structure:

1. **The Hook:** Grab the reader's attention immediately in the first 1-2 sentences. Use pattern interruption, a shocking statistic, an intense curiosity gap, or speak directly to a massive pain point. If they don't read this, nothing else matters.
2. **The Story:** Transition from the hook into a relatable story. This should build massive empathy. Show, don't just tell, that you understand their struggle because you (or a customer) have been there. Introduce an "Epiphany Bridge"—the aha moment where you discovered the solution.
3. **The Offer:** Transition from the story to the irresistible offer. Explain exactly what the product is and pitch it not as a list of features, but as the ultimate transformation they've been seeking based on the story. Create urgency or scarcity, and end with a clear, singular Call to Action (CTA).

Make the tone highly persuasive, emotional, and conversational. Do not use boring, corporate jargon.
```

## Variables
- `[CUST_AND_PRODUCT_CONTEXT]` — What you are selling, who you are selling it to, and what their primary desire/pain is.

## Example output
*(Generates incredibly persuasive, long-form sales copy perfect for Facebook Ads or email newsletters).*

## Notes
- Works best when the variables contain a highly specific customer pain point rather than a generic demographic profile.
